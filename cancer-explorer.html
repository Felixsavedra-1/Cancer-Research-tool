Display

What am I looking at? This tool downloads the real predicted 3D structure of a protein from the AlphaFold database and draws it in your browser. Cancer is often driven by mutations that change a single building block (amino acid) of a protein. Type a mutation like R175H to see exactly where on the protein it lands — mutations buried in the protein's core often break its function.
Choose a gene and press Load structure, or just hit Load to see TP53 (the most commonly mutated gene in cancer).

Why this exists

I built this to make real cancer-genomics data something anyone can explore in a browser — see a protein's true 3D shape and exactly where the mutations that drive cancer land on it. It was inspired by a young family member's fight with a rare cancer. It's a research and education tool, not medical advice.

Data: UniProt · AlphaFold DB · cancerhotspots.org. Structures are AI predictions; colour shows how confident the model is.
